Friedrich Fischer Biography

Friedrich Fischer, founder and president of Fischer Planning, is an engineer with more than 30 years of experience in the food processing industry.

For more information on Fischer Planning project experience, click here.

Background

  • 2002
    Inventor of SteriVent, the first sterile air filtration system for food, pharmaceutical and cosmetic processing and production, now selling world-wide.

  • 1995 to Today
    Self-Employed
    As consultant and engineer, designs, specifies and installs multiple food industry projects in Israel and worldwide.

  • 1986 - 1995
    Project Engineer
    EGMO (subsidiary of Neumo Ehrenberg Group), Tel Aviv, Israel
    EGMO develops, designs, manufactures and markets a wide range of stainless steel tube fittings, valves and vessels for dairies & breweries, food and cosmetic companies.

  • 1977 - 1986
    Project Engineer
    Neumo, Germany
    Neumo is one of the world’s biggest producers of valves and piping equipment. As project engineer, planned and built projects in food industry. Designed one of the first double-set valve which prevents CIP from entering the production equipment. Designed a triple piping system for cooling and heating for high viscose liquids.

  • 1974 – 1977
    Project Engineer
    Alfa Laval
    Designed many complete dairies, with capacities from 100,000-2,000,000L milk/day. Also designed one of the largest Edam and Cheddar cheese dairies in Europe, and the largest Emmenthal cheese dairies in Germany. Responsible for the relocation of many dairies from urban centers to more rural areas.

Professional Education

  • 1972 Graduate, Mechanical Engineering Program (Diplom)
    Robert Bosch Professional and Technical School, Stuttgart, Germany
